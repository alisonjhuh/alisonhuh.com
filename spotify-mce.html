The Power of Playlists:
Bringing Live Events Back Home

Ask any friend that's ever gone to a concert with me, and you'll probably hear about how I obsess over my concert collection. From looking for setlists to making sure I take the perfect cover pic, my Spotify playlists have time-and-time again helped me keep record of life's best musical moments. But that doesn't mean my playlists were made for my ears only...

Building communities,
One playlist at a time.

Keeping up with the trends, I've definitely hopped on the collaborative playlist train during quarantine.

Whether it was during my club's global online hackathon of 2,000+ participants or my 39-student programming course, collaborative playlists have easily become my favorite way to build tight-knit communities online.

Plus, I don't think there's anything as funny-yet-sad as seeing someone else awake at 4am putting a song on the playlist during the late-night hours of a hackathon.

putting users listeners first

that audience-first mentality carried over into my non-musical work as i started to explore marketing and design in college.

over the past couple years, i've worked with professional symphony orchestras, my school's graduate engineering department, and even the world' largest collegiate hackathon, so i've gotten used to presenting my work to thousands of listeners worldwide from all walks of life.
