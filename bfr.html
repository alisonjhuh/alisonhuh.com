collaborators

roles
web designer
timeline
june - august 2020
description
this past summer, i was approached by a friend of mine in the berkeley fiction review, to help redesign their wordpress site. while the main goal for this project was to help update their site's aesthetic, i took on the task of streamlining the website's navigation to make it easier to read past issues, order print copies, and find their newly revived online blog.

modernizing bfr's website

in my initial conversations with the bfr editorial team, they pitched 5 main goals this website redesign:

  1. overall redesign in aesthetics
  2. cleaner, intuitive navigation
  3. more professional layout for blogposts
  4. easier navigation of past/archived issues
  5. a "heavy overhaul in dragging it to the 21st century"
immediately, what stood out to me was the large size of the site's header, the abundance of menu options (and sub-menus), and the lack of most-recent content on the frontpage.

inspiration from other publications

before designing, i wanted to hear from the bfr team on what sources of inspiration they had in mind when "modernizing" their site.
